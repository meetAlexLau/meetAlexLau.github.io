WHO AM I?

Alex Lau is a Junior at Stony Brook University where he is majoring in Computer Science and Applied Math & Statistics. His interest stems from a high school computer science class where he discovered his fascination for coding and the intricacies of comptuters. To foster his newfound interest, he had the opportunity to intern at Caddell Prep in 2017 where he and other student interns learned front end website management for the company as well as team building in weekly company meetings. He furthered his opportunitiy when he interned at Wagner College where he got a taste of the workforce by assisting the school's Help Desk and Computer Network team in restoring and upgrading technology systems around campus.

Alex now focuses on his studies at school where he has learned and employed computer science principles such as Object Oriented Programming, Computer Systems, Data Structures, Full Stack Development, and Computer Networks. When he is not busy with academics, he is embracing is Asian background in his cultural club, Asian Students Alliance. The club hosts events to educate student participants about the history and impact of popular Asian foods and holidays, all while making it fun and interactive. Alex is responsible for brainstorming during club meetings and leading small groups of students during these events.

PORTFOLIO

Bouncy Boxes

Bouncy Boxes is class assignment that I created that focuses on inheritance and polymorphism. Some of the code was provided with the assignment. Each of the different colored boxes are subclasses to a parent Box class. These subclasses have diferent attributes, the green one is playered contorlled, blue ones decreases in size and increase in speed when in contact with the green box, red boxes stop when in contact with the green box and increase in size afterwards. Everything was brought together using JavaFx.

LeagueMeNow

LeagueMeNow is a bot on the Discord platform. I created this to quickly retrieve data from website Champion.gg, used for the popular game League of Legends. This bot uses the Discord API, used to link the bot to the code, and Puppeteer, a headless Chrome webscraper. Using Puppeteer, I was able to retrieve images from Champion.gg on specific characters using "!champ" keyword from Discor, which are processed and sent to the user.

Logo Maker

LogoMaker is a class assignment that I created using Create React App with provided class code. It employs the MERN stack to allow for persistent data between user sessions. Although locally hosted, LogoMaker provides a variety of options to create unique Logos that are updated in realtime in the edit screen.
